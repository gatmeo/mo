\qitem{%
    If $x_1,x_2$ are the roots of the equation $x^2-4x+1=0$, determine the value of $\left(x_1-\frac{1}{x_1}\right)^2+\left(x_2-\frac{1}{x_2}\right)^2$.
    }{%
    We have $\left(x_1-\frac{1}{x_1}\right)^2+\left(x_2-\frac{1}{x_2}\right)^2=x_1^2+x_2^2-4+\frac{1}{x_1^2}+\frac{1}{x_2^2}=x_1^2+x_2^2-4+\frac{x_1^2+x_2^2}{x_1^2x_2^2}=((x_1+x_2)^2-2x_1x_2)(1+\frac{1}{(x_1x_2)^2})=(4^2-2)(1+1)-4=24$. 
    }{%
    <++>
}

\qitem{%
    If $x_1,x_2$ are the roots of the equation $x^2-5x+a^2-2a+1=0$, where $a$ is a real number. Find the value of $a$, for which $x_1x_2$ is minimal.
    }{%
    $x_1x_2=a^2-2a+1=(a-1)^2$. Since $(a-1)^2\geq 0$, equalitiy holds for $a=1$. Hence when $a=1$ we have minimal $x_1x_2=0$.
    }{%
    <++>
}

\qitem{%
    Three of the roots of $x^4+ax^2+bx+c=0$ are $2,-3$ and $5$. FInd the value of $a+b+c$.
    }{%
    Since coefficient of $x^3=0$, sum of root $=0$, forth root must be $-4$. Hence the polynomail factors as $(x-2)(x-5)(x+3)(x+4)$, sub $x=1$ gives $(1+a+b+c)=(1-2)(1-5)(1+3)(1+4)=80$, $a+b+c=79$.

\qitem{%
    Let $a$ and $b$ be the roots of $x^2-3x-1=0$. Find a quadratic equation whose roots are $a^2$ and $b^2$. And compute $\frac{1}{a+1}+\frac{1}{b+1}$. (Hint: find a quadratic equation whose roots are $\frac{1}{a+1}$ and $\frac{1}{b+1}$).
    }{%
    We have $a^2b^2=(ab)^2=(-1)^2=1$, $a^2+b^2=(a+b)^2-2ab=3^2-2(-1)=11$. Hence $x^2-11x+1=0$ is the required equation.

    The eqation $(x-1)^2-3(x-1)-1=0$, or $x^2-5x+3=0$ has roots $a+1$ and $b+1$. Similarly $(\frac{1}{x}^2-5(\frac{1}{x})+3=0$, or $1-5x+3x^2=0$ has roots $\frac{1}{a+1}$ and $\frac{1}{b+1}$. Hence $\frac{1}{a+1}+\frac{1}{b+1}=\frac{5}{3}$.
    }{%
    <++>
}

\qitem{%
    Find the sum of all the roots of the equation $x^{2001}+(\frac{1}{2}-x)^{2001}=0$.
    }{%
    Using Vieta's formulas is left as an exercise. A shortcut solution is to observe that if $x$ is a root, so is $\frac{1}{2} -x$, and each such pair yields a total of $\frac{1}{2}$. There are $2000$ roots, so the total is $500$.
    }{%
