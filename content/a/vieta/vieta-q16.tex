\qitem{%
    Determine all roots, real or complex, of the system of simultaneous equations
    \begin{align*} x+y+z &= 3, \\ x^2+y^2+z^2 &= 3, \\ x^3+y^3+z^3 &= 3.\end{align*}
    }{%
    We begin by manipulating the given equations to find other useful relations between the variables.

    $ (\sum x)^2 = \sum x^2 + 2 \sum xy$

    $ 3^2 = 3 + 2 \sum xy$

    $ \sum xy = 3$

    Now using the following identity,

    $ \sum x^3 - 3xyz = (\sum x)(\sum x^2 - \sum xy)$

    $ 3 - 3xyz = 3(0)$

    $ xyz = 1$

    Now, let $ x,y,$ and $ z$ be the roots of a cubic polynomial $ P(t)$. Then, by Vieta's Formulas, we have that

    $ P(t) = t^3 - 3t^2 + 3t - 1$

    $ P(t) = (t - 1)^3$

    Therefore, $ \boxed{x = y = z = 1}$ is the only solution.
