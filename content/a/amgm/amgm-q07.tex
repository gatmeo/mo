\qitem{%
    Let $a,b,c,d \in \mathbb{R}$ such that $a^2 + b^2 + c^2 + d^2 = 1$. Prove that $(1-a)(1-b) \ge cd$.
    }{%
    Notice that the inequality is equivalent to $a^2+b^2+c^2+d^2+ab-cd\ge a+b$ and and since the $LHS\ge0$ it suffices to prove $(a^2+b^2+c^2+d^2+ab-cd)^2\ge (a+b)^2(a^2+b^2+c^2+d^2)$. Notice that $c^2+d^2-cd\ge\frac{c^2+d^2}{2}$ and now take $c^2+d^2=y$. Then $(a^2+b^2+c^2+d^2+ab-cd)^2\ge(a^2+b^2+ab+\frac{y}{2})^2$ and it suffices to show that $(a^2+b^2+ab+\frac{y}{2})^2\ge(a+b)^2(a^2+b^2+y)$. However $(a^2+b^2+ab+\frac{y}{2})^2-(a+b)^2(a^2+b^2+y)=\frac{(2ab-y)^2}{4}\ge0$
    }{%

}
