\qitem{%
Let $a,b,c$ be positive real numbers such that $abc=1$. Prove that
$a^2+b^2+c^2\geq a+b+c$.
    }{%
    Note that from
    $(a-1)^2+(b-1)^2+(c-1)^2\geq 0$, we have $a^2+b^2+c^2\geq 2(a+b+c)-3$. By AM-GM, $a+b+c\geq \sqrt[3]{abc}=3$, hence $a+b+c-3\geq 0$. So 
    \[a^2+b^2+c^2\geq 2(a+b+c)-3=a+b+c+(a+b+c-3)\geq a+b+c.\]
    }{%
