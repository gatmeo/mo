Let $O$ and $H$ denote the circumcenter and the orthocenter of $\triangle ABC$. Prove: \[ \angle BAH = \angle CAO \]
    }{%
$\angle BAH=90^{\circ}-\angle ABC$
$\angle COA=2\cdot \angle ABC ; \angle CAO= \angle ACO \implies \angle CAO= \frac{180^{\circ}-\angle OCA}2 =\frac{180^{\circ}-2\cdot \angle ABC}2=90^{\circ}-\angle ABC \implies $
\[ \angle BAH = \angle CAO \]
