\qitem{%
    A triangle has sides of length $\sqrt{13}$, $\sqrt{74}$, and $\sqrt{85}$. Find its area.
    }{%
    The Heron's Formula can also be interpreted as $\Delta=\frac{1}{4}\sqrt{(2ab)^2-\left(a^2+b^2-c^2\right)^2}$
Thus we have $\frac{1}{4}\sqrt{(2\sqrt{13\cdot 17})^2-(13+74-85)^2}=\frac{1}{4}\sqrt{4\cdot 962-4}=\frac{1}{4}\sqrt{4\cdot 31^2}=\frac{1}{4}\cdot 2\cdot 31=\frac{31}{2}$
