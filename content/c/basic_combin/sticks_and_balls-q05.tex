\qitem{%
    $7$ boys and $5$ girls are sitting around a round table. If none of the girls are sitting next to each other, how many ways can the twelve people sit?
    }{%
    First, the $7$ boys sit and arrange themselves in $6!$ ways. Then, The girls choose places in $\binom{7}{5}=\binom{7}{2}$ and arrange themselves in $5!$ ways.

    Answer: $6! \cdot \binom{7}{2} \cdot 5!$
    }{%
    <++>
}
