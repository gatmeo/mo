\qitem{%
    It is given that $(1+px)^n=1+18p^2x+15p^2x^2+$ terms involving higher powers of $x$, where $p$ is a non-zero real number and $n$ is a positive integer. Find the values of $n$ and $p$.
    }{%
    $(1+px)^n=1+{n\choose 1}px+{n\choose 2}(px)^2+\cdots =1+npx+\frac{n(n-1)}{2}p^2x^2+\cdots $.

    Comparing coeff of $x$ and $x^2$, we have
    
    $np=18p^2, \textnormal{ and }\frac{n(n-1)}{2}p^2=15p^2, n^2-n-30=0, (n-6)(n+5)=0, n=6\textnormal{ or }-5$ (rej). Sub $n=6$ to $np=18p^2$, we have $6p=18p^2, p=1/3$.
    }{%
