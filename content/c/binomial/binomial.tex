\qitem{%
    It is given that $(1+px)^n=1+18p^2x+15p^2x^2+$ terms involving higher powers of $x$, where $p$ is a non-zero real number and $n$ is a positive integer. Find the values of $n$ and $p$.
    }{%
    $(1+px)^n=1+{n\choose 1}px+{n\choose 2}(px)^2+\cdots =1+npx+\frac{n(n-1)}{2}p^2x^2+\cdots $.

    Comparing coeff of $x$ and $x^2$, we have
    
    $np=18p^2, \textnormal{ and }\frac{n(n-1)}{2}p^2=15p^2, n^2-n-30=0, (n-6)(n+5)=0, n=6\textnormal{ or }-5$ (rej). Sub $n=6$ to $np=18p^2$, we have $6p=18p^2, p=1/3$.
    }{%
    <++>
}

\qitem{%
    Find the coefficient of $x^2$ and the constant term of $(2x-1)^5(3x+1)^4$.
    }{%
    $(2x-1)^5(3x+1)^4=(-1+10x-40x^2+\cdots )(1+12x+54x^2+\cdots )$

    coefficient of $x^2=(-1)(54)+10(12)+(-40)(1)=26$, constant term $=(-1)(1)=-1$.
    }{%
    <++>
}

\qitem{%
    Find the coefficient of $x^2$ and the constant term of $\left(4-\dfrac{1}{2x}\right)^2(2+x)^7$.
    }{%
    $(4-\frac{1}{2x})^2(2+x)^7=\left(16-\frac{4}{x}+\frac{1}{4x^2}\right)(128+448x+672x^2+560x^3+280x^4+\cdots )$

    coefficient of $x^2=16(672)+(-4)(560)+\frac{1}{4}(280)=8582$, constant term $=16(128)+(-4)(448)+\frac{1}{4}672=424$.
    }{%
    <++>
}

\qitem{%
    Write down and simplify the general term in the binomial expansion of $\left(2x^2-\dfrac{d}{x^3}\right)^7$, where $d$ is a constant. 

    Given that the coefficient of $\dfrac{1}{x}$ is $-70000$, find the value of $d$.
    }{%
    ${7\choose r}2^{7-r}(-d)^rx^{14-5r}$.

    $14-5r=-1$, $r=3$. Substitute it you get $d=5$.
    }{%
    <++>
}
